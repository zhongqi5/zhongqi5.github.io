Netflix Original Film IMDB Score Visualization

 

Created by Skylar Ripley for CS416 Data Visualization at UIUC

The Dataset

The dataset contains all Netflix original films released as of June 1st, 2021, and includes their title, genre, premier date, runtime, IMDB score, and language.

The purpose of this visualization is to explore the following question: has Netflix improved at making original films over time? About the Visualization

About the Visualization

Interactive Slideshow This narrative visualization follows an interactive slideshow structure to guide users through various selected scenarios.

Scenes Three scenes have been included in this visualization using consistent template and layout. User's can navigate through the slideshow by clicking the arrows on the left/right sides of the screen, clicking the navigation dots on top, or using the left/right buttons on a keyboard.

Annotations Interesting trends are annotated in charts 1 and 2 using black font. These are meant to draw a user's attention to areas of the chart that are important to the overarching question behind the visualization.

Parameters Charts 2 and 3 include parameters that allow users to interact with the data via filtering. The scenes aim to answer a simple question and these parameters allow users to explore data in more detail.

Triggers Modifying the filters on charts 2 and 3 triggers changes to the parameters discussed above and updates the charts to reflect the event.

Number of Netflix Originals by Year

This chart shows that the number of Netflix originals has increased each year over the past several years. Note that the dataset includes originals up to June 1st, 2021, so although it looks like less originals were produced in 2021 the reality is that Netflix is on track to produce a similar number of originals to 2020.

This charts proves that Netflix is continuing to invest more and more in original films, but the questions still stands, have they improved film quality over time? Next, let's take a look at average IMDB scores for each year to move closer to an answer.

Average IMDB Score of Netflix Originals by Year

Action Adventure Animation Anime Christmas Comedy Crime Documentary Drama Fantasy Family Musical Mystery Other Romance Science Fiction Sports film War Western

This chart shows that average IMDB scores have actually steadily declined over the past several years starting in 2016. So far, Netflix original films in 2021 have received the worst scores since 2014. Check/uncheck the checkboxes to see how scores have changed for a given set of genres.

This chart ultimately answers the question of whether or not Netflix has gotten better at producing original films - on average, they haven't. That being said, it's difficult to see how all genres have scored for a given year. Next (and finally), let's take a look at average scores for each genre to provide even more insight into the data.

Average IMDB Score of Netflix Originals by Genre

2014 2015 2016 2017 2018 2019 2020 2021

This chart shows that Netflix excels at producing original films in the Animation, Crime, Documentary, and War genres. Check/uncheck the checkboxes to see how genres have scored for a given set of years.

From the past 3 charts it's clear that although Netflix is producing more and more original films the quality of these films (according to IMDB) has declined over the past several years, but specific genres have clearly outperformed others.
